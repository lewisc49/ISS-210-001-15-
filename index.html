About

The YouTube Channel, SenseCharity, does interviews on how the cost of living affects the families who have first hand experience with disabilities. Recently, they did an interview with Geordie's family. Geordie is a young boy who fights a battle with the narrowing of a passage in the body. Families like Georgies are struggling to provide the basic necessities for their loved ones. Unfortunately, without financial support to provide Geordie's feeding machine, Geordie would not be able to survive. With inflation rates at a all time high, a disadvantaged community is feeling the the financial burden of the discrimination more than ever. The diabled community struggles with hardships such as employment, fair wages, and the overall cost of living. As some disabilities prevent the ability to work, one is forced to rely on the government for financial support such as Supplemental Security Income (SSI). For most, the income ranges between $458 to $1,371 a month (ssa.gov). In addition, if a disabled bodied person is employed, the amount of money they receive would decrease. This is not a livable wage for an average able bodied person let alone someone who might have to provide materials such as wheelchairs, services like caregivers, or even additional medical treatments. In fact, a study conducted by the national disability Institution states that the poverty gap between those with and without disabilities is 15%(National Disability Institution).

Further Educational Attainment in Relation to Disabled People

The image above depicts a disabled person in a college hallway with some of their peers. While not all disabilities are surface level, this image reveals the perceived and experienced distinction that disabled people possess in comparison to their lesser affected or unaffected peers. Although disbaled people have uncontrollable limitations, they can control their future by “upskilling”. Upskilling is the practice of undergoing further educational attainment which is represented through college degrees, license, and certifications. Upskilling is conducted when an individual feels their current employment status or compensation is unsatisfactory. Upskilling disabled people to open up job opportunities | Disability Action Northern Ireland “Gerber’s study of adults with learning disabilities who were highly successful in the workforce also confirms the interconnection of personal agency and vocational achievement.60 More than half of the sample in this ethnographic study completed either a Ph.D. or M.D., and 50% reported yearly incomes of over $100,000.” Heymann, Jody, Michael Ashley Stein, and Gonzalo Moreno, Disability and Equity at Work (New York, 2014; online edn, Oxford Academic, 16 Apr. 2014), https://doi.org/10.1093/acprof:oso/9780199981212.001.0001, accessed 13 Nov. 2022.

The Socioeconomics Consequences of Cushing's Syndrome

Click here to open this PDF in a new window


This article shows the results of a study, on people affected by Cushing’s Syndrome, with regards to socioeconomic situations. The results of this study can show a general trend for other disabilities as well. This is an important aspect to take into consideration because it shows a real life experiment on the subject that is being researched. Researchers discovered that, though sick leave and disability pension were raised, annual income, education, parenthood, relationship status, and risk of depression were all negatively impacted.

Difference in Standard of Living By Income Shown Graphically

The figure above, created by The World Bank and United Nations, shows the standard of living across multiple incomes for the disabled community in comparison to those who are able bodied. The solid and bold lines represent the standard of living among these two groups and it is apparent that the disabled community’s standard of living is significantly lower than that of able bodied individuals even at equal incomes. The extra costs that disabled community has to pay regularly for necessities like health care, personal care, transportation, are all problems that able bodied individuals don’t have to face and this takes a large portion of their income.
